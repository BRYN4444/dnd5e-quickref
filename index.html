Abilities & Skills [ Collapse All ]
Strength (STR) measures bodily power, athletic training, and the extent to which you can exert raw physical force.
Dexterity (DEX) measures agility, reflexes, and balance.
Constitution (CON) measures health, stamina, endurance, and vital force. Largely passive ability.
Intelligence (INT) measures mental acuity, accuracy of recall, and the ability to reason.
Wisdom (WIS) reflects how attuned you are to the world around you and represents perceptiveness and intuition.
Charisma (CHA) measures your ability to interact effectively with others. It includes such factors as confidence and eloquence, and it can represent a charming or commanding personality.
Combat1 round = 6 seconds
Movement limited by speed
You can move at any time during your turn (before, after, or during actions).
Action 1/turn
Attack
Determine how you'd like to attack (melee, ranged, spell), pick a target within your attack's range (creature, object, location), determine modifiers (proficiency, advantage, disadvantage, penalties, bonuses), then attempt a successful attack roll to grant yourself a damage roll.
Besides proper attacks, there are a variety of things you can do that use up your action.
Bonus action max. 1/turn
You can take a bonus action only when a special ability, spell, or feature states that you can do something as a bonus action.
Reaction max. 1/round
A reaction is an instant response to a trigger of some kind, which can occur on your turn or on someone else's.
Food & Drink
Living creatures must eat and drink to sustain said life. Magic can produce food or trick the mind, but it cannot truly cure hunger or thirst. Undead creatures, however, do survive with magic alone.
Goods & Services
Whether it be though currency or bartering, you need either to do business.
Unless you want to pay every time, owning your own transportation methods could save you money in the long run.
Weapons, armor, and shields are an integral part of most combat encounters. They each vary in types and values.
There are a variety of other things to purchase, with their own unique properties.
With enough money, you can hire people to do mostly anything.
Resting & Downtime
Everyone need rest-time to sleep and eat, tend their wounds, refresh their minds and spirits for spellcasting, and brace themselves for further adventure.
Between long quests, adventurers may use this time to perform other tasks, such as crafting arms and armor, performing research, or spending their hard-earned gold.
Condition
Conditions alter your capabilities in a variety of ways, and can arise as a result of a spell, a class feature, a monster's attack, or other effect.
Lingering Injuries
All major injuries can be healed with magic. In the meantime, injuries provide penalties while adventuring.
Environmental Effects
Effects that obscure vision can prove a significant hindrance to most adventuring tasks.
The presence or absence of light in an environment creates three categories of illumination.
Some creatures have extraordinary senses that allow them to perceive their environment.
Obstacles can provide cover during combat, making a target more difficult to harm.
The world around you can provide treacherous pitfalls for the unobservant or unlucky.
Death & Prevention
If damage reduces you to 0 hit points, you fall unconscious. This unconsciousness ends if you regain any hit points.
